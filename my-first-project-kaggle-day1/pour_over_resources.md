# Pour-Over Coffee Resources

A curated collection of news, trends, communities, and learning resources for practicing pour-over coffee at home.

---

## 1. English Resources

### Communities & Discussion

#### [Reddit -- r/pourover/](https://www.reddit.com/r/pourover/)
* **Type:** Community Forum
* **Home Applicability:** **High**. A great place to ask questions, share setups, troubleshoot extraction issues (e.g., bitter or sour notes), and read recipes from other home brewers.
* **Description:** The largest online community dedicated to pour-over coffee, offering daily discussions, gear recommendations, and troubleshooting tips.

---

### News & Trends

#### [wcc.coffee (World Coffee Championship)](https://wcc.coffee/)
* **Type:** News & Competition Trends
* **Home Applicability:** **Medium**. High-level competition techniques can sometimes be complex or require specialized gear, but it is an excellent source for spotting emerging global coffee trends.
* **Description:** The official hub for the World Coffee Championships, showcasing competitive recipes, news, and modern coffee preparation standards.

#### [The Coffee Chronicler](https://coffeechronicler.com/)
* **Type:** Written Blog / Reviews
* **Home Applicability:** **High**. Ideal for intermediate brewers seeking clear guides on drippers (V60, Kalita, etc.) and understanding extraction variables.
* **Description:** An in-depth coffee blog specializing in pour-over recipes, device reviews, and analytical articles on manual coffee brewing.

---

### YouTube Channels & Video Tutorials

#### [James Hoffmann (@jameshoffmann)](https://www.youtube.com/@jameshoffmann)
* **Type:** Video Channel
* **Home Applicability:** **High**. Meticulous, structured, and easy-to-follow videos for beginners and advanced home users alike. His "Ultimate V60 Guide" is a staple.
* **Description:** Educational video guides on brewing science, step-by-step techniques, and detailed coffee equipment reviews.

#### [Lance Hedrick (@LanceHedrick)](https://www.youtube.com/@LanceHedrick)
* **Type:** Video Channel
* **Home Applicability:** **High**. Great for understanding the science of brewing (e.g., bypass, temperature, agitation) to troubleshoot and refine home extractions.
* **Description:** Science-grounded explanations of coffee extraction variables, tutorial videos, and comprehensive equipment comparisons.

## 2. Japanese Resources (日本語)

### YouTube Channels & Video Tutorials

#### [Tetsu Kasuya (@TetsuKasuya)](https://www.youtube.com/@TetsuKasuya)
* **Type:** Video Channel
* **Home Applicability:** **High**. His famous "4:6 Method" is extremely easy to replicate at home, even without a high-end gooseneck kettle.
* **Description:** YouTube channel by the 2016 World Brewers Cup Champion, sharing his world-renowned 4:6 brewing method and practical hand-drip tips.

#### [川野優馬のコーヒーチャンネル (@lightupcoffee)](https://www.youtube.com/@lightupcoffee)
* **Type:** Video Channel
* **Home Applicability:** **High**. Designed for home coffee lovers, focusing on simple adjustments (like grind size and water temperature) to make daily cups taste better.
* **Description:** Easy-to-follow hand-drip tutorials, taste adjustment tips, and reviews by the owner of Light Up Coffee in Tokyo.

---

### Written Blogs & Columns

#### [Kurasu Kyoto Journal](https://kurasu.kyoto/blogs/kurasu-journal)
* **Type:** Written Blog & Shop Updates
* **Home Applicability:** **High**. Features modern Japanese hand-drip techniques, lifestyle articles, and recipes designed for home enjoyment.
* **Description:** Coffee brewing articles and stories from a Kyoto-based specialty coffee roastery, emphasizing Japanese home brewing culture.
